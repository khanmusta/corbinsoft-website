The Challenge

Hundreds of requirements.
Multiple contractors.
One contract.

Major capital projects in oil and gas — subsea systems, FPSO topsides, refinery upgrades — are governed by tender documents that run to thousands of pages. Technical specifications, functional requirements, and regulatory obligations are embedded in Invitation to Tender (ITT) packages, vendor data requirements, and contract schedules.

When those requirements are tracked in Word and Excel, the gap between what was tendered and what is delivered only becomes visible at factory acceptance testing — or worse, at commissioning. The cost of late-stage scope gaps in offshore capital projects is measured in weeks of lost production, not change-order fees.

Tender Specification Fragmentation

Technical requirements scattered across ITT sections, data sheets, P&IDs, and project specifications with no single traceable list — leaving gaps invisible until testing.

Uncontrolled Change Orders

Scope changes issued mid-project with no traceability to original requirements. Impact assessment done by memory, not system — leading to unpriced risk and contractual disputes.

Vendor Deliverable Verification Gaps

Contractor data deliverables (calculations, drawings, test records) received without structured verification against the requirement they were meant to satisfy.

Regulatory Evidence at Handover

PSSR, COMAH, and operability verification require a complete evidence package at mechanical completion. Assembling this from project folders takes months and remains incomplete.

How Polarion ALM Solves It

Requirements discipline for capital project delivery

Apply the same engineering rigour that aerospace and medical use to manage their most critical requirements — to the tendering and delivery process in oil and gas.

Tender Specification Capture

Import and structure thousands of requirements from ITT documents, data sheets, and project specifications into Polarion work items — numbered, categorised, and owner-assigned from day one.

Multi-Contractor Scope Management

Allocate requirement subsets to individual contractors and sub-contractors. Each party sees only their scope. All deliverables are linked back to the originating tender requirement.
